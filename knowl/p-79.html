Let \(k\) be any real number, \(n\) a natural number, and suppose that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} f(x)\) and \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} g(x)\) both exist for some constant \(c\text{.}\) Then ...
  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} k = k \)
  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} [f(x) \pm g(x)] = \lim_{x \rightarrow c} f(x) \pm \lim_{x \rightarrow c} g(x) \)
  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}[k f(x)] = k \lim_{x \rightarrow c} f(x) \)
  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}\left [f(x)g(x)\right] = \left(\lim_{x \rightarrow c} f(x)\right)\left( \lim_{x \rightarrow c} g(x)\right) \)
  5. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{ \displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow c} f(x)} {\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow c} g(x)} \) provided that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} g(x) \neq 0 \)
  6. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}[ f(x)]^n = \left( \lim_{x \rightarrow c} f(x)\right)^n \)
  7. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}\sqrt[n]{f(x)} = \sqrt[n]{\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}f(x)} \) (assume \(\sqrt[n]{\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}f(x)}\) exists as a real number)
  8. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c} x^n = c^n \) (direct substitution)
  9. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow c}\sqrt[n]{x} = \sqrt[n]{c} \) (direct substitution) (assume \(\sqrt[n]{c}\) exists as a real number)
