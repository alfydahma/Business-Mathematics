Fact 4.2.1.

Total Change In \(F\): Suppose \(f \geq 0\) is continuous on the interval \([a,b]\text{,}\) and \(f\) is the rate of change of \(F\) (this means \(F' = f\text{,}\) or \(F\) is antiderivative of \(f\) ). Then the total change in \(F\) as \(x\) goes from \(a\) to \(b\) is the area between the graph of \(f\) and the \(x\)-axis from \(x=a\) to \(x=b\text{.}\)
