Solution a.1.

Let \(S(n)\) be the function that gives DDX's subscribers (in hundreds) \(n\) months after the fee was increased (that is, \(S'(n)=s(n)\)). We are asked to find \(S(7) - S(0)\text{.}\) Notice that since a loss of subscribers is expected, we should expect \(S(7) - S(0) \leq 0\text{.}\) Using TheoremÂ 4.3.1,
\begin{align*} S(7) - S(0) \amp = \int_{n=0}^{7} s(n) \, dn\\ \\ \amp = \int_{n=0}^{7} (-10n-9) \, dn\\ \\ \amp = \left(-5n^2 - 9n\right)\Biggr|_{t=0}^{7} \\ \\ \amp = -308 \end{align*}
which is less than 0, indicating a loss of subscribers as expected. As a result of increasing their monthly subscription fee from $3.25 to $6.75, DDX lost 30,800 subscribers in the first 7 months.
