Solution c.1.

As we will discuss again later, the linear revenue model will have the form
\begin{equation*} R = \textrm{(price)(quantity)}. \end{equation*}
So, the revenue model for Tyler's company is
\begin{equation*} R = 14x \text{.} \end{equation*}
In this context, the input variable is \(x\text{,}\) which represents the number or quantity of t-shirts Tyler's company produces and sells. The output variable, \(R\text{,}\) is the revenue made by Tyler's company when they produce and sell \(x\) t-shirts.
