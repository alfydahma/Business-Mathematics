Solution c.1.

Since \(M'(p)\) is the instantaneous rate of change of \(M\) (think of what happens to \(M\) as \(p\) increases by 1 unit), you would expect \(M'(p)\) to be negative since if you increase your payments by $1 per month, then the number of months it will take to pay off the card will decrease.
