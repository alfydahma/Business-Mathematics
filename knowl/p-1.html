The Euclidean plane or rectangular coordinate system is formed by two perpendicular copies of the real line intersecting at the number \(0\) of each line. Each number line is called an axis. A point in the plane, called an ordered pair, is specified by (horizontal position, vertical position). The horizontal axis is often labeled \(x\) and the vertical axis \(y\text{.}\) The point \((0,0)\) is called the origin. The plane is divided into 4 quadrants, as pictured below.
