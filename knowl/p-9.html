Suppose a line has slope $m$ and $y$-intercept $(0,b)$. If \((x,y)\) is any other point on the line then
\begin{equation*} m = \frac{m}{1} = \frac{y-b}{x-0} \end{equation*}
Cross multiplying and solving for $y$ we get
\begin{equation*} y = mx + b \end{equation*}
This is called the slope-intercept form of a line.
