Concavity and inflection points are related to the law of diminishing returns in economics. This law states that in all productive processes, adding more of one factor of production will at some point yield lower incremental per-unit returns. A point of diminishing returns refers to a point at which the optimal level of capacity is reached, where incremental increases in the input result in smaller increases in output. Mathematically, a point of diminishing returns is an inflection point through which the function increases and its derivative changes from increasing to decreasing (or equivalently, where the second derivative changes from positive to negative).
