Points on the graph of a function \(f(x)\) have the form \((x,f(x))\) where \(x\) is in the domain of \(f\text{.}\) Notice that these points have the form (domain value, range value). So, the domain values are on the \(x\text{,}\) or horizontal axis, and the range values are on the \(y\text{,}\) or vertical, axis. The points where the graph touches or crosses an axis are called intercepts.
