Example 5.2.5.

Bound-2-Read was a national chain of bookstores that failed to respond quickly to the digital age. In fact, their annual revenue was 411 million dollars at the end of the fiscal year 1996, but by the end of fiscal year 2001 was down to 296.9 million dollars. We will assume that the annual revenue for Bound-2-Read continued to decrease according to an exponential decay model.
  1. Find a base \(e\) exponential model \(R(t) = y_0e^{kt}\) that gives Bound-2-Read's annual revenue in millions dollars where \(t = 0\) is the conclusion of the 1996 fiscal year. Determine the continuous decay rate and use your model to find Bound-2-Read's annual revenue in 2004. Solution.
  2. What was the first full fiscal year where the annual revenue of Bound-2-Read was below 137.1 million dollars? Solution.
  3. Convert \(R(t)\) to a base \(b\) model of the form \(G(t) = y_0 b^t\) and identify the (actual) decay rate. Solution.
