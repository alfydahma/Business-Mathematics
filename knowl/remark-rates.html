Remark 5.2.1.

With an exponential model of the form \(f(t) = y_0 b^{t}\text{,}\) the value \(b-1\) is referred to as the growth rate if \(b > 1\) and \(1-b\) is the decay rate if \(0 < b < 1\text{.}\) These can be turned in percentages when multiplied by 100. With a model of the form \(f(t) = y_0 e^{kt}\) (or \(f(t) = y_0 e^{-kt}\)), the number \(k\) is sometimes called the continuous growth (or decay) rate (assume \(k > 0\) here). Again, this can be turned in percentages when multiplied by 100. Observe that \(b-1 = e^k - 1\) so that with the model in the form \(f(t) = y_0 e^{kt}\) (or \((t) = y_0 e^{-kt}\)) we have that \(k\) is the continuous growth (or decay) rate and \(e^k - 1\) (or \(1-e^{-k})\) is the actual growth (or decay) rate.
