With the cost per unit given as $10.75, we have
\begin{equation*} C(d) = 10.75d + F \end{equation*}
To find \(F\) we use the fact that CVL's total cost for producing \(d=98\) units is $1523.82. That is
\begin{equation*} C(98) = 10.75(98) + F = 1523.82 \end{equation*}
which gives \(F= 470.32\) so that
\begin{equation*} C(d) = 10.75d + 470.32 \end{equation*}
From the cost model above we see that CVL's fixed costs are $470.32 Observe also that the cost per unit is the slope \(m=10.75\) of \(C(d)\text{.}\) So, CVL's cost increase by $10.75 for every addtional unit of the antidresspant drug they produce.
