Solution 5.3.1.1.

We use the simple interest formula with
  • \(P = 4,000\) and
  • \(\displaystyle r= .03\)
so that our model for this problem is
\begin{equation*} I(t) = 4000(.03)t = 120t. \end{equation*}
In \(t = \frac{9}{12} = \frac{3}{4}\) years,
\begin{equation*} I\left (\frac{3}{4} \right) = 120\left( \frac{3}{4} \right) = 90. \end{equation*}
So, Maggie will pay $90 in simple interest. In 9 months she will owe her principal and interest, which is
\begin{equation*} P + I = $4000 + $90 = $4,090. \end{equation*}
