Exponential growth and decay are specific ways a quantity may change over time, \(t\text{,}\) and are modeled by functions of the form \(f(t) = y_0 e^{kt}\) or \(f(t) = y_0 b^{t}\) where
  1. \(f(t)\) = quantity or amount at time \(t\)
  2. \(f(0) = y_0e^{k \cdot 0} = y_0e^0 = y_0\) = initial amount, or
    \(f(0) = y_0b^{0} = y_0\) = initial amount for general base \(b\text{.}\)
  3. \(k\) and \(b\) are constants depending on the growth or decay rate. They are related by \(k = \ln(b)\) and \(b=e^k\text{.}\) (see Item 1 and Item 2)
  4. The model represents growth if \(k > 0\) (which is the the same as \(b > 1\))
  5. The model represents decay if \(k < 0\) (which is the the same as \(0 < b < 1\))
